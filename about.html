About Me

My name is Breawn Schoun, and I'm the Gaming Technology Developer at Children's Hospital Colorado, as well as a Computer Science PhD student in the Computer Graphics and VR Lab at the University of Colorado Denver.

In my role at Children's, I develop Extended Reality (XR) experiences for use by our patients. XR can serve a variety of clinical purposes, including distraction during procedures or transport, physical therapy, education, art/music therapy, social-emotional support, and to provide a temporary escape from the hospital. There are so many benefits to using XR in a pediatric setting, and many more yet to be discovered. It's such an exciting time to be part of this field!

My educational background is in Computer Science. I graduated from The Ohio State University in 2012, and am working towards finishing my PhD at the University of Colorado Denver. In the Computer Graphics and VR Lab at CU Denver, my research involves using thermal imaging for breathing analysis, and applying this respiratory analysis technique in VR. My primary programming languages are C#, C++ and Python.

In my free time I like to play with my two daughters, play guitar and ukulele, knit, ride bike, ride horses, play video games, and work on hobby electronics and programming projects!
